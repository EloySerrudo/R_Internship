1. Decide how many clusters you want to have
2. Place a randomly centroid in each of this cluster, it's not necesary to be one existing point
3. Next, K-Means will assign each of the data points to the closest centroid, making the clusters
4. Then, K-Means will calculate the centre of massor center of gravity for each of the clusters, that is an average of the X & Y coordinates, the centroid is not included in this calculation.
5. And then, the algorithm will move the centroids to those positions. Once they've moved, the process is repeated from step 3 the data points are reassigned the closest centroid.
6. Until there is no changes in the clusters.

K-Means++
1. The first centroid is chosen at random AMONG DATA POINTS.
2. For every data point we compute the distance to the nearest out of the already selected centroids.
3. We then use a weighted random selection to pick out the next centroid based on the distance to the existing centroids.
4. We repeat steps 2 and 3 until we have all our centroids ready and then we just apply the standard k-means clustering.
